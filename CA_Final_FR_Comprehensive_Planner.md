# CA Final FR — Comprehensive Study Planner (No Day Cap)

This version drops the artificial 15-day limit and instead paces each topic by the depth it actually needs — small/easy standards get less time, heavy-weightage and conceptually dense areas (Leases, Financial Instruments, Consolidation, Business Combinations) get more. Total span works out to roughly **35–40 study days** at a normal pace (6–7 hrs/day), but you move to the next topic only once you're solid on the current one, not when a calendar date says so.

Each topic block has three parts: **Learn** (concept/theory), **Practice** (problems), **Lock-in** (quick self-test before moving on).

---

## Phase 1: Foundations (≈5 days)

### 1. Conceptual Framework — 0.5 day
- Learn: Objectives of FS, qualitative characteristics, elements of FS, recognition/measurement
- Practice: Theory questions only (rarely numerical)

### 2. Ind AS 1 — Presentation of Financial Statements — 1 day
- Learn: Structure of FS, classification of assets/liabilities, OCI items
- Practice: 5–6 presentation/disclosure-based problems

### 3. Ind AS 2 — Inventories — 0.5 day
- Learn: Cost formulas, NRV, write-down/reversal
- Practice: 5 numericals

### 4. Ind AS 7 — Statement of Cash Flows — 1.5 days
- Learn: Direct vs indirect method, classification of activities
- Practice: 10–12 problems (high-frequency exam topic)
- Lock-in: Solve one full cash flow problem without notes

### 5. Ind AS 8 & Ind AS 10 — Policies/Estimates/Errors & Events after Reporting — 1 day
- Learn: Retrospective vs prospective application, adjusting vs non-adjusting events
- Practice: 6–8 mixed problems

### 6. Ind AS 12 — Income Taxes — 0.5 day
- Learn + Practice together: Deferred tax asset/liability computation, tax base concept
- Lock-in: 4 deferred tax sums solved independently

---

## Phase 2: Asset-Side Standards (≈6 days)

### 7. Ind AS 16 — Property, Plant & Equipment — 1.5 days
- Learn: Componentization, revaluation model, derecognition
- Practice: 8–10 problems including revaluation reversal cases

### 8. Ind AS 23 — Borrowing Costs — 0.5 day
- Learn: Qualifying asset, capitalization rate for general borrowings
- Practice: Combine with Ind AS 16 in 4–5 integrated problems

### 9. Ind AS 40 — Investment Property — 0.5 day
- Learn: Cost vs fair value model, transfers to/from investment property
- Practice: 4–5 problems

### 10. Ind AS 36 — Impairment of Assets — 1.5 days
- Learn: CGU identification, recoverable amount, allocation of impairment loss
- Practice: 8 problems, including goodwill impairment within CGU

### 11. Ind AS 38 — Intangible Assets — 1 day
- Learn: Recognition criteria, research vs development costs, amortization
- Practice: 6 problems

### 12. Ind AS 105 & Ind AS 41 — Held for Sale/Discontinued Ops & Agriculture — 1 day
- Learn: Classification criteria, measurement, presentation
- Practice: 5–6 problems combined

---

## Phase 3: Liabilities, Leases & Employee Benefits (≈5 days)

### 13. Ind AS 37 — Provisions, Contingent Liabilities & Assets — 1 day
- Learn: Recognition criteria, best estimate, onerous contracts
- Practice: 6–8 case-study style problems

### 14. Ind AS 116 — Leases — 2.5 days (high weightage — don't rush)
- Learn: ROU asset, lease liability, lessor classification (finance vs operating)
- Practice: 12–15 problems including modifications, sale-and-leaseback
- Lock-in: One full lessee + one full lessor problem solved cold

### 15. Ind AS 19 — Employee Benefits — 1.5 days
- Learn: Defined contribution vs defined benefit, actuarial gains/losses, PUC method
- Practice: 8 numericals

### 16. Ind AS 20 — Government Grants — 0.5 day
- Learn: Income vs capital approach, grant related to assets/income
- Practice: 4 problems

### 17. Ind AS 102 — Share-Based Payment — 1 day
- Learn: Equity-settled vs cash-settled, vesting conditions, fair value at grant date
- Practice: 6 problems

---

## Phase 4: Financial Instruments (≈5 days — conceptually the hardest section)

### 18. Ind AS 32 — Presentation — 0.5 day
- Learn: Liability vs equity classification, compound instruments

### 19. Ind AS 109 — Recognition & Measurement — 3 days
- Learn: Classification (amortized cost/FVOCI/FVTPL), EIR method, ECL model (12-month vs lifetime), hedge accounting basics
- Practice: 12–15 problems — this is the single most calculation-heavy standard, budget real time here
- Lock-in: Solve EIR table and ECL provisioning problems independently

### 20. Ind AS 107 & Ind AS 113 — Disclosures & Fair Value Measurement — 1 day
- Learn: Fair value hierarchy (Level 1/2/3), disclosure requirements (conceptual)
- Practice: 4–5 problems on fair value measurement

### 21. Ind AS 33 — Earnings Per Share — 0.5 day
- Learn: Basic vs diluted EPS, weighted average shares, anti-dilutive instruments
- Practice: 6–8 numericals

---

## Phase 5: Group Accounting (≈7 days — second-highest weightage area)

### 22. Ind AS 24 — Related Party Disclosures — 0.5 day
- Learn: Definition of related party, disclosure requirements (mostly theory)

### 23. Ind AS 110 — Consolidated Financial Statements — 2.5 days
- Learn: Control assessment, NCI computation, goodwill/capital reserve, intra-group eliminations
- Practice: 6–8 full consolidation problems (subsidiary only), starting simple and building complexity
- Lock-in: One full consolidation (P&L + Balance Sheet) solved without referring to solution

### 24. Ind AS 28 — Associates & Joint Ventures (Equity Method) — 1.5 days
- Learn: Significant influence, equity method mechanics, impairment of investment
- Practice: 5–6 problems

### 25. Ind AS 111 — Joint Arrangements — 0.5 day
- Learn: Joint operations vs joint ventures, accounting treatment differences

### 26. Mixed Group Problems — 1.5 days
- Practice: Combine subsidiary + associate + step acquisition/disposal in single problems (as appears in exams)
- Lock-in: One complex group problem solved end-to-end under time pressure

### 27. Ind AS 103 — Business Combinations — 1.5 days
- Learn: Purchase consideration, identifiable assets/liabilities at fair value, goodwill/bargain purchase, contingent consideration
- Practice: 6–8 problems

---

## Phase 6: Special Topics & Recent Developments (≈3 days)

### 28. Corporate Restructuring — Amalgamation/Demerger — 1.5 days
- Learn: Types of amalgamation, purchase consideration methods, accounting entries
- Practice: 5–6 problems

### 29. Integrated Reporting & Valuation of Goodwill/Shares — 1 day
- Learn: IR framework (mostly theory), goodwill valuation methods, share valuation methods (if in your syllabus)

### 30. NBFC/Bank Financial Statements (if applicable to your scheme) — 1 day
- Learn: Schedule III format differences, key disclosures

### 31. Recent ICAI Amendments — 0.5 day
- Read the latest Study Material supplement, RTP, and MTP for any amendments announced for your attempt

---

## Phase 7: Consolidation of Learning (≈4–5 days)

### 32. Full Standard-wise Revision — 2 days
- Go through your summary notes/flowcharts for all 21+ Ind AS
- Redo every problem you previously got wrong (keep a "mistake log" while studying so this is fast)

### 33. Formula & Concept Sheet Creation — 0.5 day
- Build one consolidated page covering: deferred tax steps, EPS formulas, lease ROU/liability formulas, EIR/ECL steps, consolidation working notes format

### 34. Full Mock Tests — 2 days
- Attempt 2 full-length papers (latest RTP/MTP) under strict 3-hour exam conditions
- Review errors thoroughly; revisit only the specific weak sub-topics, not entire standards

### 35. Final Buffer Day — 1 day
- Use only if a phase overran, or for last-minute revision of high-weightage standards (Leases, Financial Instruments, Consolidation, Business Combinations)

---

## How to Use This Structure
- **Don't move on until the Lock-in test passes.** If you can't solve a benchmark problem independently, repeat the Practice step rather than advancing on schedule.
- **Keep a running mistake log** from Day 1 — it becomes your single best revision resource in Phase 7.
- **Reorder if needed**: if your coaching/ICAI material groups standards differently, the phase logic (Foundations → Assets → Liabilities/Leases → Financial Instruments → Group Accounting → Special Topics → Consolidation) still holds even if you resequence individual standards within a phase.
- **Total runway**: ~35–40 working days at 6–7 hrs/day is realistic for first-time conceptual coverage; if this is a second/third revision round, you can compress each phase by roughly 40–50%.
